Table of Contents

Welcome to the AR App Technical Drawing Series. This guide is designed to help learners develop a solid foundation in technical drawing, whether for academic study, professional growth, or personal interest. The content is organized into eleven comprehensive sections, each carefully structured to introduce key concepts, practical techniques, and hands-on exercises.

01. Technical Drawing

Learn what technical drawing is, why it plays an important role in engineering, architecture, and design, and explore a brief history of its development from traditional hand-drawn methods to modern digital drafting.

Learn more: Technical Drawing

02. Materials and Tools

Discover the essential equipment needed to begin technical drawing, from pencils, rulers, compasses, and set squares to drafting boards and CAD software. This section will also highlight the importance of accuracy, maintenance of tools, and selecting the right materials for different drawing purposes.

Learn more: Materials and Tools

03. Orthographic Drawing

Gain a clear understanding of orthographic projections, which represent three-dimensional objects in two dimensions. Lessons will cover how to construct top, front, and side views accurately, as well as how to interpret and analyze drawings in order to communicate designs effectively.

Learn more: Orthographic Drawing

04. Perspective

Explore the fundamental concepts of perspective drawing. Learn about linear perspective terminology and how perspective creates the illusion of depth and space in two-dimensional drawings.

Learn more: Perspective

05. One-Point Perspective View

Discover one-point perspective, where all parallel lines converge to a single vanishing point on the horizon. This technique is perfect for drawing objects viewed directly from the front, creating depth with a single point of convergence.

Learn more: One-Point Perspective View

06. Steps on How to Create a Basic One-Point Perspective

Follow step-by-step instructions to create your own one-point perspective drawings. This practical guide will walk you through the process from setting up your horizon line and vanishing point to completing a finished perspective drawing.

Learn more: Steps on How to Create One-Point Perspective

07. Two-Point Perspective View

Master two-point perspective, where objects are viewed from an angle with two vanishing points. This technique is ideal for drawing buildings, boxes, and other rectangular objects from a corner view, creating more dynamic and realistic representations.

Learn more: Two-Point Perspective View

08. Steps on How to Create a Basic Two-Point Perspective

Learn the detailed process of creating two-point perspective drawings. This guide provides clear, sequential steps to help you construct accurate two-point perspective views, from establishing your vanishing points to adding depth and detail.

Learn more: Steps on How to Create Two-Point Perspective

09. Three-Point Perspective View

Explore three-point perspective, the most dramatic form of perspective drawing. This technique uses three vanishing points to create extreme angles and dramatic views, perfect for drawing tall buildings from ground level or looking down from above.

Learn more: Three-Point Perspective View

10. Steps on How to Create a Basic Three-Point Perspective

Follow comprehensive instructions for creating three-point perspective drawings. This advanced guide will teach you how to set up three vanishing points and construct complex perspective views with accurate proportions and dramatic angles.

Learn more: Steps on How to Create Three-Point Perspective

11. Drawing Exercise

A collection of hands-on activities to help you practice and apply what you've learned. Try out guided exercises that let you experiment with different perspective techniques, from simple geometric shapes to more complex architectural forms.

Learn more: Drawing Exercise
